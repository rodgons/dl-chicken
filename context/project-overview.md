Content Marketing for DownLow Chicken Shack

Objective
Based on your previous website audit, apply your knowledge of content marketing by creating one evergreen content piece and one ephemeral content piece for DownLow Chicken Shack. You will implement these updates on a sample website using NicePage.

As we discussed in last week's lectures, it is important to understand the audience you are targeting. Attached is a buyer persona that you must consider when creating your webpage.

Instructions
Using the insights from your website audit, open NicePage and create a website with a minimum of two pages that represents an improved version of the DownLow Chicken Shack website. Your website must include the two content types outlined below.

Task 1: Evergreen Content
Create an evergreen section on your page:

Add a clear headline (examples: "Your New Go-To Spot" or "More Than Just Chicken").
Write a short, compelling paragraph (50–100 words) explaining why DownLow Chicken Shack is the perfect choice for students like Sam. Focus on value, flavour, and convenience.
Include a small gallery of 2–3 high-quality images. For this exercise, you may use free stock photos of delicious chicken sandwiches.
This content should be timeless and remain relevant to new customers over time.

Task 2: Ephemeral Content (Example: "Weekly Special")
Create an ephemeral section on your page:

Add a feature box with a headline such as "This Week's Student Deal!" or "Limited Time: The Finals Fuel Combo."
Include an engaging image and a brief description of a fictional weekly special (for example: "A Sando, Fries, and a Drink for $15. Valid until Friday!").
Add a clear Call-to-Action (CTA) button such as "Order Now" or "Get the Deal."
This section should create a sense of urgency and feel temporary, similar to ephemeral social media content.

Submission
Publish your two-page website using NicePage and submit:

Screenshots of all pages
The public link to your website
Upload both to the Moodle dropbox for evaluation.

Time
60 minutes

If you would like to keep the website as part of your portfolio, take screenshots before deleting or unpublishing it.
